When writing or editing blog post content:

- Write casually. Short sentences, conversational flow. Use italics for emphasis rather than bold.
- Avoid sales language. Don't pitch, don't sell, don't use taglines. Just describe what happened and what it was like.
- Use em dashes sparingly (roughly 6-10 per post). No spaces around em dashes.
- Don't use phrases like "almost comically simple", "what I really wanted", "the magic happens", "it just works". These read as Claude-generated filler.
- Casual transitions are fine: "Fine!", "Sweet!", "Cool." — but don't overdo them.
- Be honest about limitations and rough edges. Don't oversell outcomes.
- When describing what Claude did, say "Claude" not "I" — the user is "I".
- Don't add calls to action ("try it yourself!", "if you want to try this...") unless there's an actual artefact to share.
- Avoid the word "straightforward" and its synonyms. If something is simple, show it rather than saying it.
- Don't end paragraphs or sections with short, punchy one-liners used as dramatic closers. This is a common AI writing pattern that doesn't match the blog's voice. Sections should trail off naturally or roll into the next thought, not land a punchline.
- Prefer medium-length sentences with natural qualifying clauses over staccato fragments. Short sentences are fine when conversational ("This works, and tends to work well.") but not when theatrical ("The silence was deafening. And then it clicked.").
- Avoid three-fragment staccato patterns like "Do the thing. Understand it. Move on." — join them into a single flowing sentence instead.
